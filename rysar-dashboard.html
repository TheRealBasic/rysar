CONTENT SERIES
Series 1 The Revival Arc ▶

Document each car as a complete story arc. Ep 1: The reveal. Ep 2–3: The attempt. Ep 4: The result. Each episode ends on a cliffhanger or question that pulls viewers to the next. Caption template: "Part [X] — [cliffhanger]"

Series 2 Budget Reality Check ▶

Running total on screen: "Spent so far: 450kr". Show trips to Biltema for parts. Real prices, real reactions. This format gets massive engagement from car people who've lived it.

Series 3 Swedish Parts Hunt ▶

Go to Biltema, Jula, scrapyards. Film the hunt. Prices in Swedish and UK terms. "This part costs £3 here and £22 back home" — that content formats extremely well.

Series 4 English Guy in Swedish Garage ▶

Cultural friction is comedy gold. Getting Swedish instructions, misreading signs, asking for help in broken Swedish. Frame it as fish-out-of-water storytelling — it broadens your audience beyond just car people.

Arrival & The Reveal
ARRIVAL DAY — SETUP & FIRST LOOK

▶

Set the stage. Document arrival, first impressions of the property and cars. This is your series pilot moment.

  • S01 Wide drone/handheld shot of the property. Slow approach to the cars. No talking — let the visuals hit first.
  • S02 Reaction to the state of the cars: "So this is what we're working with..." — genuine first impressions, no editing them out.
  • S03 Quick inventory walk-through: show ALL the cars, number them, state the challenge. "Can we get at least one running in 11 days?"
02
The Deep Inspection
ASSESSMENT DAY — DIAGNOSE ALL CARS
4 shots
▶

Go through each car systematically. This is discovery content — the audience loves finding out what problems exist.

  • S01 Interior reveal of Car 1: open the door slowly, react to what's inside. Dirt, debris, memories — all of it.
  • S02 Under-bonnet inspection: point out obvious problems, estimate difficulty. "This is going to be the nightmare one."
  • S03 Wheels, rust, bodywork inspection on all cars. Create a score for each: 1–10 for how screwed we are.
  • S04 End of day: commit to a plan. Which car first? Why? Announce it to camera like a competition show.
03
Biltema Run & Parts Chaos
SUPPLY DAY — FILM THE HUNT
3 shots
▶

The Biltema/Jula trip is content gold. Document every purchase, price comparison, and moment of confusion.

  • S01 Driving to Biltema: talk through what you need. Running budget display in post-production.
  • S02 In-store: navigate with a shopping list, find Swedish alternatives, react to prices vs UK. "This is £2?? In England this is £18."
  • S03 Parts unboxing back at the garage. Lay everything out. "Here's everything we bought for 680kr" — count it, show it.

First Attempt — Car 1 Begins
REPAIR DAY — THE REAL WORK STARTS
4 shots
▶

Full day on Car 1. Document every step, every frustration. Don't skip the boring bits — they're the authentic ones.

  • S01 Morning briefing: plan for the day, tools ready, estimate the time. Show the optimism — it makes the reality funnier.
  • S02 Timelapse of the first major task (battery, fuel system, brakes). Occasional talking head interruptions.
  • S03 The first attempt to start: build the tension, film the reaction whatever it is. This is your episode climax.
  • S04 End of day wrap: what worked, what didn't, what's next. Honest. Short. Direct to camera.

Problem Solving Day
SETBACK — DOCUMENT THE STRUGGLE
3 shots
▶

Something will go wrong. This is your best content day. The worse it goes, the better the video.

  • S01 The problem reveal: find the issue, explain it clearly, show the consequence. React honestly.
  • S02 The creative fix attempt — budget constraints force creativity. Document the solution process.
  • S03 Resolution (or escalation). Either way it's content. "We fixed it" OR "tomorrow we're trying something completely different."

4 shots
▶

  • S02 The moment of the milestone — film it from multiple angles if possible. Don't stop recording no matter what.
  • S03 The genuine celebration. Real emotions. "We did it." Keep this raw — don't clean it up in edit.

SCRIPTS & CONTENT IDEAS
// READY-TO-FILM FORMATS & EPISODE CONCEPTS
◈ EPISODE FORMATS
Format A The 60-Second Slam ▶

Structure: 0–3s Hook | 3–15s Problem | 15–50s Process | 50–60s Result or Cliffhanger

Best for: repair reveals, quick fixes, before/after. Keep cuts under 1.5 seconds. Use sound design to drive energy.

Format B The Talking Head Update ▶

Structure: Direct to camera, no B-roll. 20–40 seconds. Honest daily update — what happened, how you feel, what's next. Builds parasocial relationship. Do this once a day minimum.

Format C The Reaction Clip ▶

Film a reaction to a moment: something breaks, something works, something unexpected is found inside a car. 10–20 seconds. No setup needed. These are your viral safety net — low effort, high return.

Format D The Mini-Doc (3–5 min) ▶

Full car story from arrival to result. Use on YouTube Shorts or as a TikTok series finale. This is the hero content — produced from raw footage. Edit with chapters: Act 1 (reveal), Act 2 (struggle), Act 3 (resolution).

STANDALONE VIDEO IDEAS
  • We bought the worst car at a Swedish auction for 500kr
  • Everything wrong with a car that sat outside for 8 winters
  • Swedish mechanics react to our repair attempts (collab idea)
  • Biltema vs UK car parts — same job, price comparison
  • Starting a carbureted engine after 6 years with zero prep
  • We found £200 worth of spare parts still in the boot
  • Can you daily a car with no heat in Sweden? (October content)
  • We tried fixing a car with only tools we found in the garage
  • Rating Swedish petrol station food while we wait for parts
  • The car that probably shouldn't be driven... we drove it

SAMPLE SCRIPT — "FIRST START ATTEMPT"

OPENING (0–4s)

ON CAMERA [Stand in front of bonnet, engine bay visible] "This hasn't started since 2017. We've got a battery, some fresh fuel, and absolutely no idea if this is even possible. Let's try it."

BODY (4–45s)

SEQUENCE [Cut: battery connect] "Battery in." [Cut: fuel check] "Tank's got fuel." [Cut: key in ignition] [Pause — hold it — let the tension breathe] [Turn key] [Whatever happens — hold the camera steady]

IF IT STARTS (WIN VERSION)

REACTION [Don't cut. Let the engine run. Let the moment breathe 3–5 seconds.] "She's alive." [Pause] "Seven years. She's actually alive." Caption: "day 6. she started. i'm not okay 🥲"

IF IT DOESN'T (FAIL VERSION)

REACTION [Look at camera. Long pause.] "Right. Okay. So." [Smirk] "Tomorrow we go to the scrapyard." Caption: "day 6. she did not start. follow for part 2 👀"

EMOTIONAL STORYTELLING
// HANDLING THE HUMAN STORY WITH INTENTION
◈ THE CORE TRUTH

Assar's father left behind cars. They're not just vehicles — they're objects that carry memory, personality, and love. Rysar's job is to honour that, not exploit it. The emotional dimension of this project is what separates you from every other budget car channel. Handle it right and it becomes your most powerful differentiator. Handle it wrong and it becomes manipulative content.

WHAT TO DO
  • Let Assar lead any personal references — never push
  • Show the cars as objects of care and history naturally
  • Let quiet moments speak — a hand on a bonnet, a look
  • Reference "these belonged to someone important" once, clearly, early in the series
  • If Assar wants to share a memory, stop filming everything else and listen
  • The mechanical work IS the tribute — treat it that way
  • Getting a car running = giving something back to that memory
WHAT NOT TO DO
  • Never make grief the primary hook in captions or thumbnails
  • Don't use sad music under working footage for effect
  • Don't prompt emotional responses from Assar on camera
  • Don't reference his father more than naturally occurs
  • Never position the content as "sad story car revival"
  • Don't exploit the emotional context for engagement spikes
  • Never cut to Assar's face for a dramatic reaction without consent
HOW EMOTIONAL DEPTH BUILDS THE BRAND

DIFFERENTIATION

Most car channels are about cars. Rysar is about people who love cars. That difference — felt rather than stated — is what creates genuine audience connection and long-term loyalty.

RETENTION

Viewers stay for the story, not the specs. When people care about Assar and these cars, they watch every video in the series. Emotional investment = algorithm performance.

BRAND SAFETY

Handling grief with class makes Rysar attractive to sponsors who want association with genuine, thoughtful creators — not just another chaos channel.

◈ THE SERIES OPENER SCRIPT FRAMEWORK
SUGGESTED TONE FOR EPISODE 1 OPENING "There are about six cars on this property. They all ran at some point. None of them do right now. We're here to change that — but before we start, you should probably know why these cars are still here, and why that matters."

That's enough. Plant the seed. Don't explain it. Let viewers feel the weight without performing it. The actual story emerges across the series naturally — and that's far more powerful than stating it upfront.

Edit Priority Rule ▶

Always edit TODAY'S footage first for posting. Then batch edit "B-material" for the content backlog. Never spend more than 90 minutes on a single TikTok edit — if it's taking longer, the footage isn't the right cut yet.

Caption System ▶

Write captions in a notes file throughout the day — when you film a good moment, immediately note a caption for it. You'll never remember the feeling later. Post timing: 08:00, 14:00, 20:00 CET for max Swedish + UK reach.
